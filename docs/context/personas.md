# Teachable Personas

**Understanding who we serve, why they choose knowledge products, and how we fit into their world.**

Our personas are archetypes that represent the diverse businesses and individuals
building with us. They distill patterns in motivations, needs, and business models
into a shared language so product, marketing, and strategy align around real
customer needs and make better decisions about who we prioritize and how we
support them.

## ✅ The Knowledge Business — *primary focus*

*Knowledge is their primary business engine.*

- **Who they are:** Subject matter experts, solo educators, small education businesses.
- **Core need:** Courses/products are the main revenue stream.
- **Our platform's role:** Everything — storefront, delivery, payments, scale.
- **Origin story:** Already runs a structured, often multi-product business centered
  on their expertise (academies, training orgs, coaching institutes). May already be
  delivering courses or memberships on another platform or piecing together multiple
  specialized tools. Teachable gives them a more flexible, scalable system that
  unifies learning, commerce, and student engagement, helping them grow beyond the
  limits of their current setup.
- **Onboarding may need:** Guidance on migrating content from other platforms,
  organizing large catalogs, and advanced features like certificates or compliance.
- **How Teachable serves them:**
  - Advanced course capabilities: content drip, grading & compliance, certificates
  - Student hub: central space for catalog discovery, announcements, ecosystem links
  - Engagement suite: quizzes & assessments, comments, progress tracking
  - Globalization: one-click translations, AI subtitle translations
  - Monetization: product detail pages, order bumps, upsells, bundles
  - Payments & operations: Teachable:pay, back-office, tax handling
  - Authors: multi-instructor support to scale large catalogs
- **Sub-personas:**
  - Individual: independent experts — authors, teachers, coaches monetizing directly (e.g. [Brent Ozar](https://www.brentozar.com/))
  - Institutional: catalog builders — fitness academies, language schools, test prep companies (e.g. [Zero to Mastery](https://zerotomastery.io/))
- **Audience makeup:** Primarily B2C, uses B2B.
- **Where else are they?** Thinkific, LearnWorlds.

## ✅ The Service Amplifier — *primary focus*

*Courses are their scale lever.*

- **Who they are:** Coaches, consultants, agencies who sell services first, then add products.
- **Core need:** Productize expertise, scale beyond 1:1, upsell clients.
- **Our platform's role:** Companion system — revenue expansion, lead magnet, add-on.
- **Origin story:** Already has digital content (PDFs, decks, recordings, workshops)
  but distribution is scattered and manual. Stretched thin delivering services 1:1 or
  in small groups, and need to reclaim time. Teachable gives them a centralized,
  professional platform to package existing materials into scalable products,
  automate delivery, and free up time for higher-value work.
- **Onboarding may need:** Help repackaging existing content into scalable products,
  guidance on pricing and packaging strategies for evergreen products, and examples
  of how similar customers have streamlined delivery.
- **Sub-personas:**
  - Individual: solo coaches, consultants layering in courses
  - Institutional: small/local service businesses or agencies adding education as a companion (e.g. pilates studio, boutique consulting firm)
- **Where else are they?** Thinkific Plus, Docebo, Degreed.

## ✅ The Program Distributor — *primary focus*

*Uses Teachable as the delivery system for structured training at scale (orgs, academies, nonprofits).*

- **Who they are:** Organizations where education supports a larger mission.
- **Core need:** Deliver structured training at scale — to employees, customers, or members.
- **Our platform's role:** Infrastructure — faster, simpler alternative to a full LMS.
- **Origin story:** Already deliver education to groups, either externally through
  partners and corporate clients or internally for employees and teams. Before
  Teachable, they relied on spreadsheets, manual enrollments, or rigid LMS systems
  that made distribution clunky and hard to scale. They come to Teachable when they
  need a more flexible, professional way to streamline bulk access, automate group
  management, and track outcomes at scale.
- **Onboarding may need:** Help streamlining internal training or external program
  delivery, guidance on progress tracking at scale, and templates for reporting outcomes.
- **How Teachable serves them:**
  - Program delivery: structured courses, assessments & quizzes, content drip, grading & compliance, certificates
  - Access management: enroll individuals or groups, bulk distribution, integrate with external systems
  - Progress & reporting: track learner progress, report completions, manage outcomes at scale
  - Admin management: custom admin roles and permission layers
  - Flexibility: adaptable course formats, translation solutions, public API support
- **Sub-personas:**
  - Institutional: training companies, corporate L&D, associations, nonprofits (default)
- **Share of business:** Core to operations, but not always tied to revenue. External
  distribution → courses are a primary product and revenue driver. Internal
  distribution → courses enable training, compliance, and knowledge sharing; value is
  efficiency and consistency rather than direct sales.
- **Where else are they?** Udemy, Thinkific Plus.

## 🚫 The Course Curious — *not a current focus*

*Education is their experiment.*

- **Who they are:** Beginners, side-hustlers, or early-stage creators testing their
  first course idea. Often pre-revenue.
- **Core need:** Easy setup, templates, validation, and their "first dollar."
- **Our platform's role:** Starter kit — lowers the barrier, gives them credibility,
  helps them prove demand quickly.
- **Origin story:** Aspirational. May not have digital content yet, but they have an
  idea, expertise, or passion they want to share. Education is often an experiment, a
  side hustle, or first step toward testing whether they can turn knowledge into a
  business. Teachable gives them structure and guidance to package their first
  product, but they are responsible for getting it off the ground.
- **Sub-personas:** Side-hustlers, hobbyists, freelancers testing ideas.
- **Share of business:** Courses are 0% today, with potential to grow into a
  meaningful side hustle or even majority share if successful.
- **Where else are they?** Podia, Gumroad.

## 🚫 The Audience Builder — *not a current focus*

*Education is their monetization layer.*

- **Who they are:** Media-first creators — podcasters, YouTubers, newsletter writers,
  influencers, community leaders.
- **Core need:** Turn engaged audiences into paying customers via courses,
  memberships, and digital products.
- **Our platform's role:** Monetization engine — adds education revenue streams on top
  of their existing content/audience.
- **Origin story:** Already have a strong following through social channels,
  newsletters, or live content. Primary motivation is growing reach, building
  community, and establishing thought leadership. Education serves as the
  monetization layer on top of that audience. They come to Teachable to package their
  influence into scalable products that engage followers and generate revenue.
- **Sub-personas:**
  - Individual: influencers, podcasters, newsletter writers, YouTubers
  - Institutional: media brands, collectives, or production companies turning reach into education
- **Share of business:** Courses are typically 10–30% of revenue. Education is a
  monetization layer on top of sponsorships, ads, merch, or other streams.
- **Where else are they?** Patreon, Mighty Networks, Kajabi, Circle.
